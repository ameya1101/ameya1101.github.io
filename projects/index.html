Modelling Neutron Star Parameters using Artificial Neural Networks

A Study Oriented Project under Dr Kinjal Banerjee, Department of Physics

The Equation of State (EOS) of a neutron star enables us to deduce its structure and behaviour; the EOS is often parameterised by certain key Nuclear Matter Parameters (NMPs) which can be used to form the EOS and subsequently to deduce important properties about the star. In this project, we are attempting to develop a neural network-based model to predict neutron star properties such as its mass, radius and tidal deformability using a multivariate Gaussian distribution of the NMPs as the input. We also try to unveil correlations between nuclear matter parameters.

SugarGain

I, along with a team of 10 others, participated in the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition in 2020. Our project focused on mitigating post-harvest surcrose losses in sugarcane. As a part of the team, my work primarily involved mathematically modelling genetic ciruits and developing software. We won the gold medal and the spcial prizes for Best Software and Best Composite Part. A thorough documentation of our work can be found on our project wiki and the python package we developed as part of software contributions is listed on PyPI here.

Hybrid Quantum-Classical Computation

A research study at Infinity Labs, UST Global

We evaluated the performance of a well known quantum machine learning algorithm — the quantum support vector machine — on a dataset and compared it to its classical counterparts as well as quantum annealing approaches. This study was largely a reproduction of the results obtained by Li, R. Y. et al. (2019).
