Hi there! I'm a senior undergrad at BITS, Pilani — Goa, where I'm majoring in Physics and Computer Science. In Fall 2023, I will be starting my Ph.D. in Physics at UW-Madison.

I'm interested the development of machine learning algorithms to augment current data analysis techniques in experimental particle physics at the Large Hadron Collider. My research has an emphasis on using geometric deep learning algorithms in tandem with symmetry-equivariant architectures to (i) simulate particle physics interactions with granular calorimeter detectors; and (ii) develop accelerated particle tracking algorithms. I'm presently working on my bachelor's thesis, advised by Sergei Gleyzer, on applying denoising diffusion models to reconstruct the detector response for ttbar jets for the CMS calorimeter module.

In my free time, I enjoy going on long walks through hiking trails, setting up and maintaining aquariums (although I haven't had one for a while now!), and semi-professionally reviewing dosas.

My name is pronounced Ameya (“uh-may-yuh”).
